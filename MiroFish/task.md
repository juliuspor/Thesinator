# THESIS COPILOT
## Studyond/OST

Studyond is a Swiss startup that connects students, universities, and companies for academic projects and collaboration. We operate a three-sided marketplace where students find thesis topics from real companies, companies access talent and research capacity, and universities embed structured industry linkage into their curricula - all in one platform. Backed by Innosuisse, HSG spin-off, ETH startup.

OST - Eastern Switzerland University of Applied Sciences is a practice-oriented university with strong roots in applied research, innovation, and close collaboration with industry. With its campus in St. Gallen, OST brings academic excellence and real-world relevance together, creating an ideal environment to support entrepreneurial thinking and innovation through initiatives such as START Hack.

### Description
Every year, thousands of students start their thesis facing the same fragmented experience searching university websites for supervisors, browsing company career pages for industry topics, and cold-messaging professionals for expert interviews. The information exists - but it's scattered, unstructured, and hard to navigate. Students waste weeks on logistics instead of research.

Studyond already has all the building blocks: thousands of thesis topics from hundreds of companies, supervisors, industry experts, 20+ universities, and an AI matching engine - all connected, all live. What's missing is the intelligent experience layer that weaves it all together into a guided, AI-native thesis journey. The winning solution could directly shape our product roadmap.

### Deep Dive

**Your Mission**
We're looking for a compelling answer to the question: what does the thesis journey look like when it's built AI-first? Design an AI-powered thesis journey that guides students from *"I'm starting my thesis"* to *"I'm handing it in."* The experience should adapt to where each student is - whether they already have a topic, need a supervisor, are looking for interview partners, or are just exploring. Think of it as a co-pilot that connects the dots across topics, supervisors, companies, and experts throughout the entire thesis process.

**What We're Looking For**
A solution that is intuitive for students and solves a real pain point. This can be:
- A working prototype (technical)
- A polished concept/clickable mockup (UX-focused)
- Or anything in between

We value clever flows over tech complexity for its own sake - but we're open to being surprised by wild technical approaches.

### Technology Considerations
The solution could leverage:
- LLMs for natural language understanding and conversational flow
- Embedding models for semantic matching between thesis ideas and entities
- Autonomous agents that proactively search, suggest, and act on the student's behalf
- Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) for contextual responses
- Context accumulation across interactions to build a rich thesis profile over time

### Resources Provided
- Full context layer: domain knowledge, platform data model, audience profiles, brand guidelines - optimized for use as LLM context
- Mock datasets: supervisors, company topics, expert profiles, sample thesis ideas
- The Studyond team - around all weekend for questions, feedback, and brainstorming

### Judging Criteria
What matters most is whether your solution genuinely helps a thesis student. Does it make sense? Would someone actually use it? As a rough guide: about 40% of our evaluation focuses on how well the solution actually works - is it practical, does it solve a real pain point, is the experience coherent? Another 30% goes to creativity and innovation - did you approach the problem in a way that surprised us or made us think differently? The final 30% is about feasibility - is this technically sound, could it realistically be built and scaled? Your submission can lean technical, lean design, or be a mix of both - we're completely open on the form. But regardless of which direction you take, it should be grounded in reality: technically feasible and practically useful.
